introduction

Hello, my name is Leland. Thank you very much for taking the time to view my portfolio. I am an aspiring software engineer presently midway through a software engineering program with Thinkful.

I was born and raised in Nebraska. I currently work as a Police Sergeant in Portland, Oregon. I have worked in law enforcement for ten years with a demonstrated history of developing and implementing solutions to complex problems, collaboration with stakeholders from a wide variety of backgrounds, and meeting demanding expectations with minimal supervision.

about me

I attended college at the University of Nebraska-Omaha. I was awarded both a regent's scholarship and the prestigious Walter Scott, Jr. scholarship. After studying computer science for about two years I decided to pursue other interests. I became a police officer to help people in need. I have worked as a police officer and sergeant for a combined ten years. I hope to apply many of the transferable skills I have learned to a new career as a software developer, including communication, teamwork, and critical analysis.

Perhaps the most valuable things in any job are the people with whom one works. In my job search, I hope to find a team of excellent programmers as enthusiastic about coding as myself, with great senses of humor, and a strong work ethic. While working through the curriculum of the software engineering program in which I am enrolled I have monitored a Discord and Slack server of my peers. I actively look to help others with their problems when they arise because it reinforces my skills and builds a positive cultural norm around mutual support. When I arrive at a problem I strive to test my abilities and solve it myself. When I have exhausted my resources I craft a question as specifically as possible about the issue I'm facing and note the efforts I've already made.

I am passionate about technology and problem-solving. It is immensely satisfying to me when I can craft a piece of code that performs a useful task or take a broken piece of code and make it work. As I work through my Bootcamp curriculum I am continually noticing and admiring how different websites and apps interact with users. I look forward to developing such solutions myself and continuing my lifelong journey of learning in software development. Here are some of the languages, frameworks, skills, and concepts I have studied:

  • JavaScript ES6
  • React.js
  • HTML 5
  • CSS 3
  • Node.js
  • Express
  • PostgreSQL
  • GitHub
  • Agile Methodology (CSM Certification)

I compliment those skills with my leadership, communication, and problem-solving abilities developed over my ten years in law enforcement.

When I'm not coding I love to be outdoors or out on the town. I'm into hunting, fishing, camping, hiking, architecture, art, and theatre.
